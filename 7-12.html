7 - 12 years

Health Care

7 - 8 years

  • Flu Vaccine (Yearly)
  • HPV Vaccine (2 doses, 6-12 months apart)

Flu Vaccine (Yearly): Influenza vaccine is recommended annually to protect against seasonal flu viruses. It helps prevent flu-related illnesses, hospitalizations, and complications

HPV Vaccine (2 doses, 6-12 months apart): Human Papillomavirus (HPV) vaccine is recommended for both boys and girls to protect against HPV infections, which can lead to various cancers, including cervical, anal, and throat cancers.

9 - 10 years

  • Tdap Vaccine (Booster)
  • Meningococcal Vaccine (1st dose)

Tdap Vaccine (Booster): Tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis (whooping cough) vaccine booster is recommended at this age. It provides continued protection against these diseases.

Meningococcal Vaccine (1st dose): Meningococcal conjugate vaccine helps protect against meningococcal disease, which can cause meningitis and bloodstream infections. The first dose is typically given around age 11-12.

11 - 12 years

  • MenACWY Vaccine (Booster)
  • HPV Vaccine (if not completed earlier)

MenACWY Vaccine (Booster): Another dose of meningococcal conjugate vaccine (MenACWY) may be recommended at this age for continued protection against meningococcal disease.

HPV Vaccine (if not completed earlier): If the HPV vaccination series wasn't completed earlier, the remaining doses should be administered. It's essential to complete the series for optimal protection against HPV-related diseases.

Yearly (Ages 7-12)

  • Flu Vaccine (Yearly)

Flu Vaccine (Yearly): Annual flu vaccination continues to be recommended to protect against seasonal influenza viruses.
