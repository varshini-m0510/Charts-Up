Health Care

Puberty

13 - 14 years

  • HPV Vaccine (if not completed earlier), MenACWY Vaccine (Booster)

HPV Vaccine (if not completed earlier): Human Papillomavirus (HPV) vaccine is recommended for both boys and girls to protect against HPV infections, which can lead to various cancers, including cervical, anal, and throat cancers. If not completed earlier, the series should be initiated or completed during these ages.

MenACWY Vaccine (Booster): Meningococcal conjugate vaccine helps protect against meningococcal disease, which can cause meningitis and bloodstream infections. A booster dose is typically recommended during adolescence for continued protection.

15 - 16 years

  • MenACWY Vaccine (Booster), Tdap Vaccine (Booster)

MenACWY Vaccine (Booster): Another dose of meningococcal conjugate vaccine (MenACWY) may be recommended at this age for continued protection against meningococcal disease.

Tdap Vaccine (Booster): Tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis (whooping cough) vaccine booster is recommended during adolescence to maintain immunity against these diseases.

17 - 18 years

  • MenACWY Vaccine (Booster), MenB Vaccine (Series)

MenACWY Vaccine (Booster): If not already received, adolescents should receive a booster dose of meningococcal conjugate vaccine for continued protection.

MenB Vaccine (Series): Serogroup B meningococcal vaccine helps protect against meningococcal disease caused by serogroup B bacteria. It's typically given as a two-dose series, with the second dose administered 1-2 months after the first dose.

Yearly (Ages 13-18)

  • Flu Vaccine (Yearly)

Flu Vaccine (Yearly): Annual influenza vaccination is recommended to protect against seasonal flu viruses. It helps prevent flu-related illnesses, hospitalizations, and complications.
